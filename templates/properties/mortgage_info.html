Mortgage Plans

Understanding how mortgages work is the first step towards owning your dream home.

What is a Mortgage?

A mortgage is a type of loan specifically designed to help you purchase a property, whether it's a house, flat, or land. Instead of paying the full price upfront, you borrow money from a bank or financial institution and repay it over a set period (usually 10 to 30 years) in monthly installments that include interest.

The property itself serves as collateral for the loan. This means if you're unable to keep up with payments, the lender has the right to recover their money through the property. Once you fully repay the mortgage, you own the property outright.

Why Are Mortgages Important?

  • Makes homeownership accessible: you don't need the full purchase price saved up before you can buy a home.
  • Builds equity over time: every payment you make increases your ownership stake in the property.
  • Predictable payments: fixed-rate mortgages give you a known monthly cost, making budgeting easier.
  • Asset growth: property values generally appreciate over time, so your investment grows.
  • Tax benefits: in many cases, mortgage interest payments are tax-deductible.

Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN)

The Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN) is the apex mortgage institution in Nigeria, established by the Federal Government to provide affordable housing finance to Nigerians. It plays a central role in making homeownership a reality for working Nigerians through the National Housing Fund (NHF).

If you contribute to the NHF scheme (a mandatory 2.5% deduction from your monthly salary), you become eligible to apply for a mortgage loan at a significantly reduced interest rate, currently as low as 6% per annum, compared to commercial bank rates of 20% or more.

What FMBN Offers

  • NHF Mortgage Loans: low-interest home loans for contributors to the National Housing Fund.
  • Home Renovation Loans: funding to renovate or upgrade your existing property.
  • Construction Financing: loans for building your own home from the ground up.
  • Rent-to-Own: a scheme that lets you live in and gradually pay for an affordable home.
  • Estate Development Loans: financing for developers building affordable housing estates.
